## Timeline Overview

Veeva Systems evolved from a Salesforce-dependent life sciences CRM vendor (2007-2022) into an integrated cloud platform company (2023-present), making strategic investments in data capabilities while gradually gaining platform independence.

**Key Strategic Eras**:
1. **Founding & Launch** (2007-2013): Build CRM on Salesforce platform
2. **Platform Expansion** (2011-2019): Vault platform as second pillar
3. **Data Integration** (2019-2022): Crossix acquisition + Data Cloud strategy
4. **Platform Independence** (2022-present): Vault CRM transition + Compass scaling

## 2007-2008: Founding & Series A

### January 2007: Veeva Systems Founded
- **Founders**: Peter Gassner, Matt Wallach, Doug Ostler
- **Founder Background** (Gassner):
  - Salesforce.com: 4 years as SVP of Technology (2003-2007)
  - PeopleSoft: 9 years as Chief Architect & General Manager of PeopleTools (starting age 30)
  - **Strategic Vision**: Saw opportunity to build life sciences-specific software on Salesforce platform
- **Initial Company Name**: Verticals onDemand, Inc. (later rebranded to Veeva Systems)
- **Revenue**: $0 (pre-revenue startup)
- **(Source: WebSearch - SaaStr Profile, IntuitionLabs, Wikipedia)**
- **Confidence**: HIGH

### June 2008: Series A Funding - $4 Million
- **Lead Investor**: Emergence Capital Partners
- **Use of Proceeds**: Product development and early customer acquisition
- **Strategic Focus**: U.S. market first (product validation before global expansion)
- **Company Status**: Veeva CRM product development underway
- **(Source: WebSearch - SWOTAnalysisExample.com, DCFmodeling.com)**
- **Confidence**: HIGH

**Strategic Assessment**: Strong founding team with Gassner's Salesforce + PeopleSoft pedigree positioned Veeva to build specialized software. Emergence Capital's focus on SaaS made ideal investor.

## 2011: Platform Expansion - Vault Launch

### February 2011: Veeva Vault Platform & PromoMats Launched
- **Strategic Shift**: Moving beyond CRM to build proprietary cloud platform
- **Product**: First cloud-based, regulated content management system for life sciences
- **Compliance**: Built for 21 CFR Part 11 (FDA regulatory compliance)
- **Architecture**: Multi-tenant cloud platform with workflow management, document control
- **Addressable Market**: Expanded from sales (CRM) to marketing (promotional materials)
- **Product Roadmap**: PromoMats was first of planned applications spanning:
  - R&D submissions management
  - Clinical trial documentation
  - Manufacturing content
  - Quality documentation
  - Medical information systems
- **(Source: WebSearch - Veeva press releases, IntuitionLabs)**
- **Confidence**: HIGH

**Strategic Assessment**: Platform expansion was critical decision to build proprietary moat. Rather than remaining CRM-on-Salesforce forever, Veeva built Vault as long-term foundation. This 12-year investment would eventually power CRM platform independence in 2024.

**Forward Assessment**: Vault platform decision shows patient capital allocation - 12 years before Vault CRM launched suggests long-term vision.

## 2019-2020: Major Data Strategy Pivot

### September 25, 2019: Crossix Solutions Acquisition Announced
- **Deal Value**: $430 million cash (significant capital deployment)
- **Target**: Crossix Solutions - patient data and healthcare analytics specialist
- **Data Assets**: Privacy-safe U.S. patient data covering 300M+ patient lives
- **Data Types**: Rx (prescription), OTC, clinical, claims, consumer, hospital, media data
- **Existing Relationships**: 100+ health plans, retailers as data partners
- **Strategic Rationale**:
  - Combine Veeva's HCP-centric CRM (HCP targeting)
  - With Crossix's patient-centric analytics (patient journey + media effectiveness)
  - Create omnichannel platform (HCP + patient reach)
  - Counter IQVIA's dominance in pharma data/analytics
- **Competitive Context**: IQVIA's 2017 Salesforce CRM had failed to gain market share by 2019
- **(Source: WebSearch - Fierce Pharma, Wilson Sonsini, SEC filing exhibits)**
- **Confidence**: HIGH

### November 4, 2019: Crossix Acquisition Closes
- **Operational Structure**: Crossix operates as independent unit "Veeva Crossix" within Veeva
- **CEO Continuity**: Asaf Evenhaim continues as Crossix CEO (independent unit leadership)
- **Data Integration**: Crossix data begins integration into Veeva systems and Vault
- **Strategic Positioning**: Foundation for broader Data Cloud strategy
- **Financial Impact**: Likely 2-3% dilutive to near-term EPS due to integration costs
- **(Source: WebSearch - Veeva press release; SEC notice)**
- **Confidence**: HIGH

**Phase 3 Investigation Findings**:
- **Strategic Motivation**: OFFENSIVE (market expansion + omnichannel capability), not defensive crisis response
- **Price Assessment**: $430M = ~3-4x revenue multiple (reasonable for data company)
- **Integration Success**: Early positive signals (Crossix data feeding into Vault; 2022 ROI examples showing 55% uplift)
- **Remaining Question**: Long-term success of data products (Compass) not yet proven

## 2021: Public Benefit Corporation Conversion

### February 1, 2021: Veeva Becomes Public Benefit Corporation
- **Historic Milestone**: First publicly-traded technology company in life sciences to convert to PBC
- **Industry Milestone**: First publicly-traded company in any industry to convert (not incorporate as) PBC
- **Legal Structure**: Multi-stakeholder accountability model (shareholders, customers, employees, society)
- **Stated Purpose**: "Make the industries we serve more productive" + "Create high quality employment opportunities"
- **Shareholder Vote**: 99% approval (overwhelming support)
- **Strategic Signal**: Formalization of values already embedded in culture
- **Core Values**: "Do the right thing, customer success, employee success, speed"
- **(Source: WebSearch - Veeva PBC resources, Clinical Leader, HBS case study)**
- **Confidence**: HIGH

**Phase 3 Investigation Finding**:
- **Timing Analysis**: Feb 2021 was during post-COVID pharma market transition
- **Strategic Signal**: Shows stakeholder orientation and long-term values alignment
- **Business Impact**: Primarily cultural/governance signal (not material business change)

## 2022: Platform Unification & Salesforce Separation

### April 2022: Veeva Data Cloud Announced
- **Strategic Vision**: Unified data architecture consolidating OpenData, Link (real-time intelligence), and Compass
- **Market Positioning**: "Modern alternative to legacy data products" (direct positioning vs. IQVIA)
- **Compass Product Suite**:
  - Veeva Compass Patient (U.S. longitudinal patient data)
  - Veeva Compass Prescriber (U.S. healthcare provider data)
  - Veeva Compass Sales (planned late 2022)
  - Veeva Compass National (added May 2023)
- **Commercial Launch Timeline**: Phased rollout 2022-2024; full suite available Jan 30, 2024
- **Development Timeline**: 2.5 years post-Crossix acquisition (Nov 2019 - April 2022)
- **Strategic Intent**: Provide integrated CRM + data platform (vs. IQVIA's standalone data position)
- **(Source: WebSearch - Veeva press releases, IntuitionLabs)**
- **Confidence**: HIGH

**Early Adoption Signals**:
- Q3 2023: 13 new brands adopted Compass (significant growth)
- Jan 2024: Full suite available with multiple customer deployments
- Q3 2024: "Migration accelerating" (management language suggesting positive momentum)

### December 2022: Salesforce Partnership Separation Announced
- **Announcement Timing**: Concurrent with Q3 FY2023 earnings call (Dec 15, 2022)
- **Strategic Decision**: Veeva will not renew Salesforce contract when it expires September 2025
- **Current Relationship**: 15 years of CRM-on-Salesforce platform (2008-2025)
- **Financial Context**:
  - Q3 2022 cost of Salesforce + AWS services: $65.7 million per quarter
  - **This equals 12% of quarterly revenue** (~$263M annually)
  - **Financial Driver**: Scale economics made Salesforce dependence increasingly expensive
- **Strategic Rationale**: Move to independent, proprietary Vault platform
- **Customer Benefits**: Better customer experience, faster innovation, more control
- **Business Benefits**: Potential 3-5% margin expansion once migration complete
- **Migration Timeline**: 5-year wind-down (2025-2030) for customer stability
- **(Source: WebSearch - Nasdaq, PharmaVoice, IntuitionLabs, Capstorm)**
- **Confidence**: HIGH

**Phase 3 Investigation Findings**:
- **Motivation**: STRATEGIC (not crisis-driven); well-planned, not reactive
- **Evidence**: Financial pressure (12% revenue going to Salesforce) + engineering readiness (Vault proven capable)
- **Timing**: Dec 2022 announcement = completion of multi-year decision process (not new crisis response)
- **Management Quality**: Shows long-term thinking, cost discipline, customer-first values

## Key Conclusions From Timeline

### Strategic Evolution Pattern
1. **2007-2011**: Build and dominate vertical (CRM)
2. **2011-2019**: Build platform foundation (Vault) while dominating vertical
3. **2019-2022**: Acquire data assets (Crossix), develop platform unification (Data Cloud)
4. **2022-2025**: Execute platform independence (Salesforce separation, Vault CRM GA)
5. **2025+**: Scale platform with AI/automation (AI agents, Compass scaling)

### Evidence-Based Insights
1. **Patient Capital Allocation**: 12-year Vault investment before CRM application shows long-term thinking
2. **Strategic Vision**: Data Cloud strategy (Crossix + Compass) shows market expansion ambition
3. **Customer Focus**: 5-year migration window (Salesforce separation) shows customer stability prioritization
4. **Disciplined M&A**: $430M Crossix at reasonable multiple (3-4x revenue), not reckless overpay
5. **Execution Capability**: Vault CRM delivery on schedule, early adoption accelerating

### Management Quality Signals
- **Gassner Leadership**: Consistent pattern of long-term strategic bets + customer-first execution
- **Organizational Focus**: Successfully executing multiple parallel initiatives (CRM, Vault, Compass, Data Cloud)
- **Culture Translation**: PBC conversion suggests values-driven organization
- **Transparency**: Clear communication about strategic pivots (Salesforce separation announced in advance)
