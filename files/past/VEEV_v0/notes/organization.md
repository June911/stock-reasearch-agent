# Veeva Systems - Organizational Research

**Ticker:** VEEV
**Research Date:** November 18, 2025
**Researcher:** Organization Specialist

## Leadership Team

Peter Gassner co-founded Veeva Systems in 2007 and has served as CEO since January 2007, bringing 30+ years of experience in enterprise software transitions across mainframe (IBM), client-server (PeopleSoft), cloud (Salesforce), and industry cloud software. He holds a BS in Computer Science from Oregon State University and previously served as Chief Architect and General Manager at PeopleSoft and SVP of Technology at Salesforce. Tim Cabral served as CFO from 2010 to 2020, growing the company from ~$10 million to over $1 billion in revenue through the 2013 IPO, and has been appointed Interim CFO. Additional key executives include Josh Faddis (General Counsel and Secretary), Catherine Allshouse (TechOps and global operations), Thomas D. Schwenger (President and COO), and Vivian Welsh (global employee success programs). Recent leadership transitions include CMO Stacey Epstein's resignation in July 2024, with Nitsa Zuppas transitioning from CMO to President and Chief of Staff during 2024.

## Board Composition

Veeva's board consists of 11 directors with approximately 82% independence (9 independent directors). Gordon Ritter serves as Chairman. Notable independent board members include Mark Carges (former eBay CTO), Dr. Mary Lynne Hedley (25+ years biotech/pharma executive experience), Priscilla Hung (former Guidewire President & COO), and Marshall Mohr (former Intuitive Surgical CFO/EVP healthcare technology). Tim Cabral, co-founder Matt Wallach, and CEO Peter Gassner also serve as directors. The board has established standard committees: Audit Committee, Compensation Committee, Cybersecurity Committee, and Nominating and Governance Committee, all with independent chairs per NYSE listing standards. Average board tenure is 6.3 years.

## Ownership Structure

Insider ownership stands at approximately 11% of outstanding shares, with CEO Peter Gassner as the second-largest shareholder holding 7.6-10.1% of company shares (varying by filing date), representing approximately 16.14 million shares. Institutional investors control approximately 81-82% of the company. The Vanguard Group is the largest institutional shareholder with 8.9% of shares outstanding, followed by BlackRock with 5.9%. The top 19 shareholders collectively own 51% of the company, indicating no single shareholder has dominant control. Veeva maintains a single-class share structure with equal voting rights for all shareholders.

## Compensation & Alignment

CEO Peter Gassner's total compensation reached $172.44 million, comprised of 0.3% salary ($475,000 as of April 2025) and 99.7% variable compensation including a massive stock option grant valued at $172 million with five-year annual vesting through February 2030 and a performance condition requiring the stock price to reach $236.90 for 60 consecutive trading days. The compensation is intended to cover CEO compensation through fiscal 2030 with a post-exercise holding period extending through 2032. Other named executive officers also emphasize equity compensation heavily through stock options and RSUs, with CEO shareholding requirements set at three times annual base salary and other executives required to hold stock valued at one times annual base salary. Frederic Lequient ($2.47M), Frank Defesche ($1.12M), and Jonathan Faddis ($494K) are among other top-paid executives.
