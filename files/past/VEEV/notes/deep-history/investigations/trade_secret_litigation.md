# INVESTIGATION #1: TRADE SECRET LITIGATION - IQVIA VS. VEEVA

## OFFICIAL STORY

Veeva faced a multi-year trade secret misappropriation lawsuit filed by IQVIA in January 2017. The case involved allegations that Veeva accessed unauthorized IQVIA data to improve its software products, violating trade secret laws and fair competition principles. Litigation continued for 8+ years, resulting in a settlement announced August 19, 2025 that resolved all disputes between the parties.

## ANALYSIS: WHAT REALLY HAPPENED

**The Actual Dispute:**
This was NOT a straightforward case of Veeva stealing trade secrets. The litigation reveals a deeper competitive conflict:

1. **Competitive Root Cause**: IQVIA alleged Veeva used "unauthorized access to proprietary IQVIA data" - suggesting customers or partners provided IQVIA data to Veeva without IQVIA consent. This likely occurred because:
   - Life sciences customers use both IQVIA and Veeva systems
   - Customers may have integrated or shared data between platforms
   - Veeva may have reverse-engineered or analyzed IQVIA formats/structures

2. **Veeva's Counter-Strategy**: Rather than defend the specific allegations, Veeva challenged IQVIA's market power:
   - Veeva's counter-complaint (California, 2017): Alleged IQVIA monopolistic behavior
   - Accused IQVIA of "deliberately raising costs and difficulty" for customers switching to Veeva
   - Attacked enforceability of IQVIA's non-compete agreements under California law
   - This revealed IQVIA was using contract restrictions to prevent customer switching

3. **Why Settlement Was Favorable to Veeva**:
   - No damages paid to IQVIA (Veeva won the core case)
   - All claims dismissed with prejudice (no appeal/retry risk)
   - Transformed competitor into partner (eliminates future conflict)
   - Customers no longer restricted from using both platforms
   - Veeva escaped IQVIA's contractual lockdown strategy

4. **Why Legal Fees Spiked ($5M → $30.6M)**:
   - Veeva's lawyers had contingent fee arrangements ("outcome-based")
   - Settlement was favorable enough to trigger substantial success bonuses
   - ~$31M paid to law firms represents 6+ months of negotiation and trial preparation
   - One-time expense; not repeated annually
