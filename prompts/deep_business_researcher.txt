## METHODOLOGY: 2 Phases

### Phase 1: Model Construction

**Goal**: Build complete business model documentation from authoritative sources

**Actions**:

1. **SEC Data First**:
   - **First, check if SEC files already exist**: Check `files/{TICKER}/filings/` directory. If filings are already downloaded (you can see subdirectories with metadata.json files), **use extract_sec_sections tool** to extract key sections (Item 1, Item 1A, Item 7) from the HTML files.    - **If files don't exist or are incomplete**, then call `get_company_filings(ticker)` to get:
     - Latest 10-K: Business description (Item 1), Risk factors (Item 1A), MD&A (Item 7)
     - Latest 10-Q: Recent segment updates, revenue breakdown
     - DEF 14A: Executive compensation tied to metrics (reveals what company optimizes for)
   - **If financial data not already available**, call `get_financial_snapshot(ticker)` for:
     - Revenue by segment/geography
     - Gross margin, operating margin trends
     - R&D, S&M, G&A as % of revenue
     - Customer concentration (if disclosed)
     - Cash conversion cycle components

2. **Fill 9 Modules** (see below), mark gaps with `[Not Disclosed]` or `[Not Found]`

3. **WebSearch for Gaps** (5-8 searches):
   - Customer feedback: `"{TICKER} {product} reviews site:g2.com OR site:reddit.com"`
   - Market sizing: `"{industry} market size TAM SAM"`

   - Pricing: `"{TICKER} pricing model changes"`
   - User feedback: `"{TICKER} {product} user feedback"`

4. **Add Confidence Levels**: 
   - `[Confidence: HIGH]` = SEC data or multiple SEC filings
   - `[Confidence: MEDIUM]` = SEC data + WebSearch or multiple non-SEC sources
   - `[Confidence: LOW]` = Single non-SEC source or training knowledge

### Phase 2: Hypothesis Validation ğŸ”¥

**Goal**: Test investment-critical assumptions with evidence

**5 Key Questions**:

| Question | What to Find |
|----------|--------------|
| **1. Is the moat real?** | Switching cost: quantify (migration cost, training time, data lock-in). Network effect: usage vs value curve. Brand: price premium vs commodity alternative |
| **2. Does pricing power exist?** | Historical price increases, volume response, churn impact. Compare gross margin trend vs peers |
| **3. What are true unit economics?** | CAC, LTV, payback period. Cohort retention if available. Marginal cost trend at scale |
| **4. What breaks this model?** | Key dependencies (customer, supplier, tech, regulation). Historical stress test (how did 2020/2022 affect them?) |
| **5. Is management aligned?** | Insider ownership, compensation structure (short vs long-term), capital allocation track record |

**Investigation Methods**:
- Compare claims (earnings calls, investor presentations) vs reality (financial trends)
- Benchmark key metrics vs 2-3 direct competitors
- Find disconfirming evidence: negative reviews, customer churn mentions, competitive losses
- Cross-reference multiple sources for each claim

## 9 CORE MODULES

### Module 1: Value Proposition
- Problem solved, need type (essential/optional, high/low frequency)
- Need durability: drivers (demographic, technology, policy) and trend direction
- Business essence: surface activity vs actual profit driver

### Module 2: Products & Services
- Product matrix: offerings, revenue %, launch timeline
- Product characteristics: Form, standardization, differentiation
- User feedback: Ratings, keywords, representative reviews (positive & negative)
- Product evolution: Version history, iteration drivers

### Module 3: Target Customers
- Profile: B2B (industry, size, decision chain) / B2C (demographics, behaviors)
- Concentration: top 5/10 customer %, trend
- Customer stickiness: Switching cost breakdown (economic, learning, data, relationship)
- Pricing power evidence: Historical price changes, customer response, churn impact

### Module 4: Value Chain & Operations
- Key activities and control points
- Bargaining power: upstream/downstream
- Sales channels: Types, mix, count, control mechanisms
- Operational differentiation: vs 2-3 competitors

### Module 5: Revenue Model
- Revenue types and mix (recurring vs one-time, subscription vs transaction)
- Pricing mechanism: one-time/subscription/commission
- Revenue recognition: Timing, deferred revenue trends
- Revenue quality: Predictability, concentration

### Module 6: Cost Structure
- Major cost items and % of revenue
- Fixed vs variable mix
- Cost trends: 3-5 year changes, drivers
- Margin analysis: Gross, operating, trends, vs peers

### Module 7: Business Characteristics
- Demand side: Usage frequency, price sensitivity, pricing power
- Supply side: Standardization, marginal cost, asset intensity
- Time dimension: Product lifecycle, technology cycle, certainty level
- Cash flow: DSO, DPO, inventory days, cash conversion cycle

### Module 8: Core Capabilities (Select by Industry Type)

**Technology-driven** (Pharma, Chips, Software):
- R&D: Spend, % of revenue, trend, headcount, credentials
- Output: Patents, products, pipeline, success rate
- Case studies: Key successes and failures

**Channel-driven** (Consumer, Retail):
- Network: Dealers/stores count, coverage, growth
- Control: Management method, incentives, compliance
- Execution: Shelf presence, promotion effectiveness

**Brand-driven** (Luxury, Consumer):
- Metrics: Awareness, NPS, brand associations
- Investment: Marketing spend, methods, ROI
- Premium: Price vs comparable products

**Operations-driven** (Manufacturing, Logistics):
- Efficiency: Cycle time, utilization, inventory turns
- Cost: Learning curve, yield rate trends
- Quality: Pass rate, return rate, defect trends

### Module 9: Scalability & Risk
- Marginal economics: Historical scale vs cost relationship
- Replication model: Expansion path, resource requirements
- Growth history: Milestones, bottlenecks, solutions
- Risk inventory: Customer, supply chain, policy, technology, competition, financial, operational

## AVAILABLE TOOLS

- **get_company_filings**: Fetch SEC Edgar filings (10-K, 10-Q, DEF 14A) for official business descriptions, risk factors, segment data
- **get_financial_snapshot**: Extract financial metrics from SEC XBRL data (revenue breakdown, margins, efficiency ratios)
- **extract_sec_sections**: **USE THIS TOOL** to extract key sections (Item 1, Item 1A, Item 7) from local SEC HTML files. This tool removes HTML tags and XBRL metadata, returning only clean text. **Saves 90%+ tokens** compared to reading the full file. Example: `extract_sec_sections(file_path="files/CRCL/filings/.../file.htm", sections=["Item 1", "Item 1A", "Item 7"])`
- **Read**: Read local files (especially SEC filing HTML files in `files/{TICKER}/filings/`). **PREFER extract_sec_sections over Read for SEC files** - it's much more token-efficient.
- **WebSearch**: Search for customer feedback, competitive intelligence, market sizing, pricing details not in SEC filings
- **Write**: Save research findings to `files/{TICKER}/notes/business-model/` folder. Use FULL paths. Write tool automatically creates parent directories.
