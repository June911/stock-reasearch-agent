<research_focus>
When researching business model, focus on:

1. **Revenue Model** (1-2 paragraphs)
   - What are the primary revenue streams? (product sales, subscriptions, licensing, services)
   - Revenue breakdown by segment (% of total revenue)
   - Revenue breakdown by geography (if significant)
   - Recent revenue growth rates and trends
   - Gross margins and unit economics (if available)
   - Customer concentration (enterprise vs consumer, key customers)

2. **Competitive Advantages / Moats** (1-2 paragraphs)
   - What makes this company defensible?
   - Network effects, switching costs, brand, regulatory barriers?
   - Proprietary technology, patents, or trade secrets?
   - Cost advantages or economies of scale?
   - Examples: NVIDIA's CUDA ecosystem, Apple's ecosystem lock-in, Amazon's logistics network
   - How durable are these advantages?

3. **Market Position & TAM** (1 paragraph)
   - What is the Total Addressable Market (TAM) size?
   - What is the company's market share?
   - Who are the top 3-5 competitors?
   - How does the company differentiate from competitors?
   - Is the market growing, stable, or declining? At what rate?

4. **Business Quality Indicators** (1 paragraph)
   - Key financial metrics: revenue growth, profit margins, ROIC, cash flow
   - Capital intensity (CapEx requirements)
   - R&D spending as % of revenue
   - Customer retention rates or churn (if available)
   - Pricing power and recent price changes
</research_focus>

GOOD (Specific with Sources):
NVIDIA generates revenue primarily through GPU sales across three segments: Data Center (80% of Q3 FY2024 revenue, $14.5B), Gaming (17%, $2.9B), and Professional Visualization/Automotive (3%). Data Center revenue grew 279% YoY driven by AI accelerator demand (H100, A100 chips). Gross margins reached 75% in Q3 2024, up from 56% in prior year, reflecting pricing power in AI chips and favorable product mix shift (Source: NVIDIA Q3 FY2024 Earnings Report, November 2023).

NVIDIA's competitive moat stems from its CUDA software ecosystem - a parallel computing platform with 4+ million developers trained on it. This creates massive switching costs: enterprises investing in CUDA-optimized models (PyTorch, TensorFlow integration) face high re-training costs to move to AMD or Intel alternatives. The moat is reinforced by a 10-year head start in AI chip design and 90%+ market share in AI training chips (Source: Jon Peddie Research GPU Market Report, 2023).
