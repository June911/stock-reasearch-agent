### Phase 3: Deep Investigation ğŸ”¥
**Approach**: 100% evidence-based - NO guessing allowed

**Triggers** (investigate when you see):
- Gap between stated strategy and financial outcomes
- Unusual margin trends (expansion or compression without clear cause)
- Customer concentration changes
- Pricing power claims without evidence
- "Moat" assertions that seem generic
- Business model transitions or pivots
- Competitive dynamics shifts

**Investigation Protocol**:

1. **Economic Reality Check**:
   - Unit economics: What does it actually cost to acquire/serve/retain a customer?
   - Pricing power test: Historical price increases, customer response, churn impact
   - Switching cost validation: Find actual examples, quantify where possible

2. **Customer Deep Dive**:
   - Scrape reviews (G2, Capterra, Glassdoor for B2B culture signals)
   - Find case studies, customer testimonials (what do they actually value?)
   - Look for churn signals: customer complaints, competitive losses mentioned in filings

3. **Competitive Dynamics**:
   - Compare financials with 2-3 direct competitors
   - Map competitive moves: pricing changes, product launches, market entries
   - Assess barriers: What actually stops competition? (not what company claims)

4. **Business Model Stress Test**:
   - What breaks this model? (concentration risk, technology disruption, regulation)
   - Historical stress: How did model perform in downturns?
   - Dependency mapping: Key suppliers, customers, technologies, people

5. **Apply "3 Whys" Analysis**:
   - Why do customers buy? (stated vs revealed preference)
   - Why do customers stay? (inertia vs value)
   - Why can't competitors replicate? (real barriers vs temporary advantages)

**Output**:
- Investigation reports: `files/{TICKER}/notes/business-model/investigations/{topic}.md`
- Each report: Claim â†’ Evidence â†’ Reality â†’ Investment Implication

## 9 CORE MODULES (Phase 1 Skeleton Structure)

### Module 1: Value Proposition
- Problem solved / Need satisfied
- Need characteristics: Essential vs optional, frequency, willingness to pay
- Need durability: Drivers (demographic, technology, policy), trend direction
- Business essence: Surface activity vs actual profit driver

### Module 2: Products & Services
- Product/service matrix: All offerings, revenue %, launch dates
- Product characteristics: Form, standardization, differentiation
- User feedback: Ratings, keywords, representative reviews (positive & negative)
- Product evolution: Version history, iteration drivers

### Module 3: Target Customers
- Customer profile: B2B (industry, size, decision chain) / B2C (demographics, behaviors)
- Customer structure: Total count, top 5/10 concentration, industry distribution
- Customer stickiness: Switching cost breakdown (economic, learning, data, relationship)
- Pricing power evidence: Historical price changes, customer response, churn impact

### Module 4: Value Chain & Operations
- Complete value chain: End-to-end process map
- Key nodes: Critical activities, company control level, bargaining power up/downstream
- Sales channels: Types, mix, count, control mechanisms
- Operational differentiation: vs 2-3 competitors

### Module 5: Revenue Model
- Revenue sources: Categories, %, pricing method (one-time/subscription/commission)
- Revenue recognition: Timing, deferred revenue trends
- Revenue quality: Recurring %, predictability, concentration

### Module 6: Cost Structure
- Cost breakdown: Major items, %, fixed vs variable
- Cost trends: 3-5 year changes, drivers
- Margin analysis: Gross, operating, trends, vs peers

### Module 7: Business Characteristics
- Demand side: Usage frequency, price sensitivity, pricing power
- Supply side: Standardization, marginal cost, asset intensity
- Time dimension: Product lifecycle, technology cycle, certainty level
- Cash flow: DSO, DPO, inventory days, cash conversion cycle

### Module 8: Core Capabilities (Select by Industry Type)

**Technology-driven** (Pharma, Chips, Software):
- R&D: Spend, % of revenue, trend, headcount, credentials
- Output: Patents, products, pipeline, success rate
- Case studies: Key successes and failures

**Channel-driven** (Consumer, Retail):
- Network: Dealers/stores count, coverage, growth
- Control: Management method, incentives, compliance
- Execution: Shelf presence, promotion effectiveness

**Brand-driven** (Luxury, Consumer):
- Metrics: Awareness, NPS, brand associations
- Investment: Marketing spend, methods, ROI
- Premium: Price vs comparable products

**Operations-driven** (Manufacturing, Logistics):
- Efficiency: Cycle time, utilization, inventory turns
- Cost: Learning curve, yield rate trends
- Quality: Pass rate, return rate, defect trends

### Module 9: Scalability & Risk
- Marginal economics: Historical scale vs cost relationship
- Replication model: Expansion path, resource requirements
- Growth history: Milestones, bottlenecks, solutions
- Risk inventory: Customer, supply chain, policy, technology, competition, financial, operational
