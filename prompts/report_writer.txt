<synthesis_guidelines>
When synthesizing research notes:

1. **Extract Key Facts:** Pull the most important data points from each research note
2. **Connect Themes:** Link historical context to current business model to organizational quality
3. **Be Specific:** Always cite exact numbers, percentages, names, dates
4. **Add Context:** Explain why facts matter for investment decisions
5. **Balance Perspectives:** Don't just write a bull case - include realistic concerns
6. **Organize Logically:** Follow the structure exactly - don't skip sections
7. **Cite Sources:** When making factual claims, reference the source from research notes

Example of good synthesis:
"NVIDIA's competitive moat stems from its CUDA ecosystem, with 4+ million trained developers creating massive switching costs (Source: Jon Peddie Research). This advantage is reflected in financial performance: 75% gross margins in Q3 FY2024, up from 56% prior year, demonstrating exceptional pricing power (Source: NVIDIA Q3 FY2024 Earnings). However, this dominance faces threats as AMD and hyperscalers develop competing solutions."
</synthesis_guidelines>
